Honors and Awards

1st Place Award in the Heatstroke Preventions Challenge The 5th International Conference on ABC
September 9, 2023
At the 5th ABC Conference's Heatstroke Prevention Challenge, I was honored with the 1st place award and a prize of 0.1M JPY for my innovative contributions to advancing heatstroke risk prediction. My project centered on developing a robust predictive model that integrates physiological data. By combining these elements, I was able to achieve a significant improvement in prediction accuracy and responsiveness, creating a system capable of providing early warnings to help mitigate heatstroke risks. This award highlights my dedication to leveraging data science and machine learning for public health initiatives.
1st Place Award in the 3rd Nurse Care Activity Recognition Challenge ACM UbiComp/ISWC
September 26, 2021
I was awarded 1st place, along with 0.1M JPY prize money, in the 3rd Nurse Care Activity Recognition Challenge for my innovative approach to complex activity recognition in healthcare. My solution focused on leveraging accelerometer data to accurately identify various nurse care activities, a critical step in optimizing patient care workflows and reducing staff fatigue. By employing advanced machine learning techniques, I developed a method capable of managing the intricate, multi-step nature of caregiving tasks. This award and recognition reflect my dedication to enhancing healthcare efficiency through data science, supporting both patient outcomes and the well-being of medical staff.
Best Paper Award (Bronze) in the joint 10th ICIEV Conference and 5th ICIVPR Conference Virtual Conference | Online
August, 2021
I received the Best Paper Award (Bronze) for my work on applying focal loss to address class imbalance in nurse care activity recognition. This paper presented a novel adaptation of focal loss within a residual network framework, significantly enhancing the model's ability to accurately classify underrepresented activities. By refining activity recognition in complex healthcare environments, this research contributes to more responsive and precise predictive systems. This award underscores the innovative impact of my work in addressing key challenges in healthcare analytics, combining technical rigor with real-world applications to improve patient care processes. Manuscript
2nd Place Award in the 2nd Nurse Care Activity Recognition Challenge HASCA Workshop/UbiComp-2020
September 12, 2020
I earned the 2nd Place award in the 2nd Nurse Care Activity Recognition Challenge, where I developed a highly effective solution for accurately recognizing complex nurse care activities. By analyzing accelerometer data and employing advanced machine learning techniques, I created a model that could handle the nuances and variability of caregiving tasks, capturing essential details within diverse nursing workflows. This award acknowledges the model’s effectiveness in not only improving the precision of activity recognition but also its potential to support healthcare efficiency and reduce caregiver fatigue. The recognition highlights my commitment to bridging technology and healthcare, delivering data-driven solutions that enhance patient care and create supportive environments for medical professionals.
